Red Planet


The surface of Mars is mostly comprised of iron-rich minerals. What you see is ACTUALLY RUST! Iron rusts when exposed to water and oxygen, causing the rusty-red appearance of Mars.

Mars has weather similar to Earth in that it can have clouds, wind, and snow.

Yet, the atmosphere here is too thin to support life.

Rover

Who me?

I do all sorts of work here, but my favorite way to pass the time is using my laser!

Curiousity was launched in 2011. It’s about the size of a car, and weighs almost 2,000 lbs.


Its primary mission is to find out if Mars could have supported alien microbial life.

Hand


A drill on Curiousity's hand grinds rock samples into a powder, then pours the samples into its body for analysis. Instruments inside the body identify what the samples are made of.

Also on the hand is a brush to remove dust, a soil scoop, a camera for close-up views, and two sample analysis tools:


MAHLI - Mars Hand Lens Imager for analyzing rock


APXS - Alpha Particle X-Ray Spectrometer for chemical measurements

7 cameras

are equipped to Curiousity's mast and head.


The ChemCam on the rover's "forehead"

fires a laser

at rocks and soil from a distance to analyze the composition of the material it just vaporized.

Olympus Mons

The shield volcano

Built almost entirely from fluid magma flows, its lava flows far and wide.

This is the largest

volcano

in the solar system and it is still active.


Lava flows for a long distance before hardening, and eruptions are frequent.

Olympus is the size of Arizona.


Olympus Mons is about 100 times larger than that of Mauna Loa, Earth's largest volcano in Hawaii.


In fact, the entire chain of Hawaiian islands (from Kauai to Hawaii) would fit inside Olympus Mons!

Water

Not a drop to spare

My primary mission here is to find out if mars can support life, or if it ever did in the past.

Evidence

Canyons, layered sediment, frozen ice caps, frozen water beneath the surface, hot springs, sustained precipitation, rocks showing exposure to water, and ancient stream beds.


With a thin atmosphere, and low gravity, the water has all but evaporated.

Closer Look

Dark streaks of salty water appear on the surface of Mars seasonally.

Click the image to take a closer look.


Curiousity rolled through an ancient stream bed shortly after touching down in August 2012, and it has examined a number of rocks that were exposed to liquid water billions of years ago.
